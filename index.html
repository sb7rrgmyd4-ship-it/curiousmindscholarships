Our Story

Twelve Years of Opening Doors

Founded in 2012 by a coalition of Nigerian business leaders, academics, and philanthropists, The Curious Minds Foundation was established with a single conviction: that financial circumstance should never determine intellectual destiny. Over the past twelve years, we have partnered with 48 universities, funded over 1,200 scholars, and disbursed more than ₦50 million in scholarships, grants, and support packages across Nigeria and the African continent.

Our mission is to eliminate financial barriers to education for talented but under-resourced students. Our vision is an Africa where every brilliant mind — regardless of postcode, family income, or circumstance — has unrestricted access to world-class education and the mentorship to transform that education into lasting impact.

Excellence

We fund those who refuse to be ordinary. We seek students who push boundaries and set new standards.

Equity

We reach those the system has overlooked. First-generation students and underserved communities are our priority.

Empowerment

We build scholars, not just beneficiaries. Mentorship, networks, and leadership development are core to every award.

Requirements

Who Can Apply?

General Requirements

Nigerian citizen or African resident with valid ID
Minimum CGPA of 2.75/5.0 (varies by scholarship)
Currently enrolled in or admitted to an accredited university
Demonstrated financial need OR exceptional academic merit
No active disciplinary record from your institution
Must not currently hold another full scholarship
Age: 16–30 years at time of application

Required Documents

Current academic transcript (officially stamped)
Admission letter or current enrollment confirmation
Two reference letters (one academic, one character)
Personal statement essay (minimum 200 words)
Proof of financial need (bank statement or income declaration)
Valid government-issued ID (NIN, passport, or voter's card)
Passport photograph (recent, clear background)

Got Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

Any Nigerian citizen or African resident aged 16–30, currently enrolled in or admitted to an accredited university, with a minimum CGPA of 2.75/5.0 (higher for some scholarships). Financial need is required for the Equity & Access Grant; exceptional merit is required for the Founders Merit Scholarship.
You may apply for one scholarship per cycle. Our admissions panel will assess your profile and may consider you for an alternative award if you do not qualify for your first choice.
All complete applications — including passed tests and submitted documents — are reviewed within 6–8 weeks of the application deadline. You will be notified exclusively via the email address used during registration.
Yes. All four scholarships are renewable each academic year provided you meet the minimum CGPA threshold and submit a renewal application before the cycle deadline. Renewal is not automatic.
You must be enrolled or have a valid admission offer from an accredited Nigerian university at the time of application. Gap year students awaiting admission may not apply in that cycle.
Tuition is paid directly to your institution. Living allowances are disbursed weekly to your registered bank account. Grants (laptop, research, project fund) are paid as one-time transfers after documentation verification.
The Foundation currently accepts applications from students enrolled in accredited Nigerian universities regardless of nationality. Non-Nigerian applicants must hold a valid residence permit and provide proof of enrollment.
Selected scholars attend a mandatory onboarding session (virtual or in-person), sign a Scholar Agreement, and are paired with a mentor. Disbursements begin within 2 weeks of onboarding completion.
